AFA Member Portal

Tendering support for AFA members — delivered by McMillan Tendering

McMillan Tendering is pleased to support members of the Australian Furniture Association (AFA) with practical, professional tendering services. Support is available whether you need assistance with a live tender, want to strengthen your internal tendering capability, or require a clear win strategy for a major opportunity.

Below are the core services available to AFA members. Each service links to further detail on my website.

Services available to AFA members

Three practical service pathways

Bid / Tender Production Services

Expert assistance for your tender submissions

Expert assistance with the preparation and coordination of tender submissions. Support can range from targeted input on specific sections through to end-to-end tender management, ensuring responses are structured, complete and compliant.

Typical support includes

  • Tender requirement analysis and submission planning
  • Drafting and review of tender responses and schedules
  • Coordination of inputs and final submission preparation

Read more about Bid / Tender Production Services

Tendering Process Improvement

Enhancing your tendering capabilities

Support to improve how your organisation approaches tendering, with a focus on efficiency, consistency and quality. Ideal for businesses that tender regularly and want to reduce risk while improving outcomes.

Typical support includes

  • Review of existing tender processes and tools
  • Improvement of templates, content libraries and governance
  • Practical frameworks to support repeatable, scalable tendering

Read more about Tendering Process Improvement

Win Strategy Formulation

Developing winning strategies

Structured development of win strategies for higher-value or more competitive tenders. This service focuses on positioning, differentiation and aligning your response to the buyer’s key drivers.

Typical support includes

  • Facilitated workshops and structured analysis
  • Competitive positioning and key message development
  • Clear strategy to guide tender preparation
